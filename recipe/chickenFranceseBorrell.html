Chicken Francese
Borrell

Ingredients:

  • 4 skinless, boneless, chicken breasts (about 1.5 pouds) All-purpose flour, for dredging
  • Salt and pepper
  • 3 tbs water
  • 1/4 cup of EVOO
  • 1/2 lemon, with rind, cut in thin rounds
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine, such as pinot grigio
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • 2 tbs unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped flat-leaf parsley

Recipe:

  1. Poud the chicken breast with a flat meat mallet until they are about 1/4 inch thick
  2. Mix flour, salt, and pepper in shallow platter and dredge chickn in mixture
  3. Heat oil over medium-high flame in large skillet, once hot add cuttlets and fry for 2 minute on each side
  4. Toss lemon slices into pan and cook for 1-2 minutes (intil fragrent)
  5. Add wine, broth, and lemon juice - simmer for 5 minutes to reduce the sauce slightly
  6. Roll the butter in some flour and add it to the skillet (this will thicken the sauce)
  7. Stir to incorporate and dissolve the flour, reduce the heat to medium-low and return chicken to pan
  8. Place lemon sliced on top of the cutlets - simmer gently for 2 minutes to heat the chken through
  9. Season with salt and pepper and garnish with chopped parsley

Chicken Francese History

Growing up, there would be a battle for the Chicken Francese, with Mike (Biagioli) always trying to eat it all. This tradition continues to today whenever this dish is made, and Mike is around.
