Matzo Ball Soup
Rosich

Ingredients:

  • 1 4–5-lb. chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  • 1 pound chicken necks, and/or backs
  • 2 large yellow onions, unpeeled, quartered
  • 6 celery stalks, cut into 1 inch pieces
  • 4 large carrots, peeled, cut into 1 inch pieces
  • 3-4 sprigs flat-leaf parsley chopped fine
  • 1 tbs dill weed
  • Manichevitz matzo ball mix

Recipe:

  1. Bring all ingredients and 12 cups cold water to a boil in a very large (at least 12-qt.) stockpot. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until chicken breasts are cooked through, about 20 minutes
  2. Place dill weed and parsley in tea ball (brewing basket) and hang in the water while soup is cooking
  3. Transfer breasts to a plate (remaining chicken parts are strictly for stock). Let breasts cool slightly, then remove meat
  4. Shred meat and set aside
  5. Continue to simmer stock, skimming surface occasionally, until reduced by one-third, about 2 hours. Strain chicken stock through a fine-mesh sieve (or a cheese cloth) into a large saucepan; discard solids with the exception of the carrots which should be returned to the stock. You should have about 8 cups
  6. Add the shredded chicken breasts back to the stock Prepare matzo mix according to package instructions
  7. Scoop out 2-tablespoonful portions matzo ball mixture and, using wet hands, gently roll into balls
  8. Add matzo balls to boiling broth and reduce heat so broth is at a gentle simmer (too much bouncing around will break them up). Cover pot and cook matzo balls until cooked through, 20–25 minutes

Matzo Ball Soup History

This dish was originally made by Mike's father's Grandmother. The recipe has since been pased down and is made by Mike's mother during the winter months.
